Sunil Chhetri

India’s goal-machine extraordinaire.

Biography

Sunil Chhetri (born 3 August 1984, Secunderabad, now Telangana) is an Indian professional footballer who plays as a forward for Bengaluru FC and the India national team. He’s India’s most capped player and leading goalscorer, ranked among the very top international scorers globally. Widely regarded as the greatest footballer India has produced.

Education/Journey

As the son of an Indian Army officer, Chhetri attended multiple schools across India—including Bahai School (Gangtok), Army Public School (Delhi), and others—before graduating from Asutosh College, Kolkata.

He left studies during 12th grade to represent India at the Asian School Championships in Kuala Lumpur in 2001. Soon after, Mohun Bagan signed him and his professional career took off.

Abroad, he had stints/trials with clubs like Queens Park Rangers and Sporting CP B, overcoming work-permit and adaptation challenges.

Projects & Achievements

Sunil’s trophy cabinet is overflowing: he’s won multiple national and regional cups (Nehru Cup 2007, 2009, 2012; SAFF Championship in 2011, 2015, 2021, and 2023; 2008 AFC Challenge Cup) and played a key role in elevating India’s football stature. At club level, he has numerous league and cup titles with Bengaluru FC and others.

On the individual front, Chhetri has been named AIFF Player of the Year seven times, received the Arjuna Award (2011), Padma Shri (2019), and became the first footballer to win India’s top sporting honor, the Khel Ratna Award (2021). His goal tally places him fourth in the all-time international goals list, behind legends like Ronaldo, Messi and Ali Daei. After announcing retirement in mid-2024 from international play, he returned to help out in 2025.
